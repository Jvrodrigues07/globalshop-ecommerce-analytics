# 📊 Marketplace Analytics: From Transactional Data to Business Intelligence

End‑to‑end business analytics project transforming raw marketplace transactional data into strategic operational insights using SQL, Python, and Power BI.

---

# 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes marketplace performance to understand:

- How revenue grows over time  
- What drives business expansion  
- How customer behavior influences sales  
- Whether logistics performance impacts operational reliability  

The objective is to convert raw transactional data into actionable business intelligence that supports strategic decision-making.

---

# 🧩 Business Problem

Although the company demonstrates continuous order growth, several strategic questions remain unanswered:

- Is growth driven by new or returning customers?  
- Which products truly sustain revenue?  
- What are customers’ preferred payment methods?  
- Does delivery performance affect marketplace reliability?  

This project aims to answer these questions through a structured analytics pipeline.

---

# 🗄 Dataset

Brazilian Olist public e-commerce dataset containing:

- Orders  
- Customers  
- Products  
- Payments  
- Logistics information  

The dataset simulates a real multi-seller marketplace operating nationwide.

---

# 🏗 Project Architecture

SQL → Python → Power BI → Executive Business Insights

---

# 🧮 Stage 1 — SQL (Data Extraction & Structuring)

SQL was used to extract and structure raw relational data into analysis-ready datasets.

Key processes:

- Data joins across transactional tables  
- Revenue calculation  
- Order aggregation  
- Time-based grouping  
- Data validation  

This stage transformed raw operational data into structured analytical outputs.

---

# 🐍 Stage 2 — Python (Data Preparation & Aggregation)

Python (Pandas) was used to refine and prepare structured data for business intelligence consumption.

Key processes:

- Data cleaning and validation  
- Handling missing values  
- Revenue correction and verification  
- Monthly KPI aggregation  
- Average Order Value calculation  
- Exporting structured CSV for BI layer  

Output: Analytical dataset ready for dashboard development.

---

# 📊 Stage 3 — Power BI (Business Intelligence & Storytelling)

Power BI was used to develop an executive dashboard and extract strategic insights.

## KPI Development

- Total Revenue  
- Total Orders  
- Average Order Value (AOV)  
- Revenue Trend Analysis  
- Seasonality Analysis  

DAX measures were implemented for accurate KPI calculations and performance tracking.

## Dashboard Structure

### Page 1 — Executive Dashboard
- KPI summary cards  
- Monthly revenue evolution  
- Order volume trends  
- AOV performance  

### Page 2 — Business Insights
- Strategic business questions  
- Analytical answers  
- Growth model interpretation  

Power BI enabled visual storytelling and executive-level interpretation of operational data.

---

# 📈 Key Analyses Performed

## Growth Analysis  
Monthly revenue demonstrates steady expansion driven primarily by customer acquisition rather than retention.

## Customer Behavior  
Customer identifier limitations suggest the business model is acquisition-driven rather than loyalty-driven.

## Product Concentration  
Revenue is concentrated within a limited number of high-impact products, indicating dependency on specific catalog segments.

## Payment Behavior  
Credit card transactions dominate, indicating convenience-driven purchases, while boleto usage reflects more cautious buyers.

## Logistics Performance  
Average delivery time is approximately 12.5 days, with over 90% of orders arriving within the promised deadline, demonstrating strong operational reliability.

---

# 💡 Business Insights

- Growth is acquisition-driven, not loyalty-driven  
- Marketplace performance depends on high-impact products  
- Customers heavily rely on credit-based purchasing  
- Delivery performance is predictable and operationally reliable  

---

# 🏁 Executive Conclusion

The marketplace demonstrates sustainable and scalable growth supported by customer acquisition and operational consistency. Revenue expansion is driven by volume growth and supported by reliable logistics performance rather than customer retention dynamics.
